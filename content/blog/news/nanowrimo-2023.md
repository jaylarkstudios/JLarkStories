Greetings all! I'm excited to introduce my goals for NaNoWriMo this year!

Traditionally, one would try to complete a first draft of a novel, or write 50,000 words during the month of November. However, I'm taking a more unconventional approach this year. For one thing, I'm writing the next chapter of Aww, Feathers! which is a comic rather than a novel. Plus, the chapter is already mostly written. It is, however, in need of some revision and polish. By the end of this month, I hope for the script to be ready to begin turning into comic form!

The other major wrinkle for me this year is that I'm going in for shoulder surgery later this month. This will, understandably, make a number of activities, including typing, difficult for a while. I do want to make some effort to make a little bit of progress each day. But I promise not to do anything my doctor tells me to avoid, and I'll pay close attention to how I'm feeling and take a break if needed. But if I'm feeling up to it, I may simply write using my phone, by voice, or even just typing with one hand.

For both of these reasons, I'll be focusing on a daily minute goal rather than word count. So without further ado, here are my goals for NaNoWriMo this year:

- Spend 25 minutes per day organizing my notes for Aww, Feathers! including world-building details, character bios, plans for upcoming chapters, etc.
- Spend 25 minutes per day working on the script for my next comic chapter.
- On Sundays, take the day off from comics writing, and do 25 minutes of personal writing instead, such as journaling, exploring topics of personal interest, or even just free-writing.

I also thought it would be fun and also keep me accountable to share my progress at the end of each day, so I've devised a calendar/grid system for keeping track:

- ⬛️ is just a filler so the days line up correctly.
- 🔳 is a day I haven't finished yet.
- 🟩 means I hit all my writing goals for the day.
- 🟨 means I hit some of my writing goals for the day.
- 🟥 means I didn't do any writing that day.
- 🟦 means I went above and beyond my writing goals for the day.

I'll post my "progress grid" on my socials, then as each day goes by, it will gradually be filled in. Feel free to use or adapt this system if you like it.

Best wishes to everyone participating in NaNoWriMo this year!
