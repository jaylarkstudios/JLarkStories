I was challenged recently to come up with at least twenty exercises, each of  which can be completed in one day and would help me towards accomplishing one of my goals, and then complete at least one of them every day. The order in which they are completed doesn't matter, and they can even be done multiple times.

The goal I chose is to improve  my character drawing. To help keep me accountable, I'll be posting the results of my daily drawing exercises here for patrons to see! If you notice me go a day without posting one, feel free to call me out on it! (Though I plan to take Sundays off from uploading and then post two on Mondays.)

Here are the exercises I came up with:

1. Draw a five-angle turnaround of one character’s head (front, three-quarter, side, three-quarter-back, and back views).
2. Draw the six basic expressions of one character’s face (joy, sadness, anger, fear disgust, surprise).
3. Draw a five-angle turnaround of one character’s body (front, three-quarter, side, three-quarter-back, and back views).
4. Draw a character’s head at four odd angles (Examples: bowed, raised, tilted, top-view, underside).
5. Draw the same character with four different body types (Examples: starved, skinny, fit, medium, muscular, plump, obese).
6. Draw the same character at four different ages (Examples: infant, toddler, child, teen, young adult, middle age, elderly, ancient).
7. Fill a page with drawings of hands.
8. Fill a page with drawings of feet/paws.
9. Fill a page with drawings of action poses.
10. Fill a page with drawings of the upper body (chest, shoulders, back, torso, etc.)
11. Fill a page with drawings of the lower body (hips, legs, backside, tail, etc.)
12. Fill a page with drawings of fabric/drapery.
13. Fill a page with drawings of species-specific clothing/accessories.
14. Fill a page with reference drawings of a particular animal species.
15. Gender-bend or species-swap two existing characters.
16. Draw a full-body sketch of a completely new, randomly generated character.
17. Fill a page with characters drawn in an exaggerated cartoony-style.
18. Fill a page with characters drawn in a minimalistic/very simplified style.
19. Complete a hyper-realistic sketch of a character or animal.
20. Complete an extreme foreshortened sketch of a character.
21. Complete a grayscale shading study on a character with a specific light source (Examples: front-lit, back-lit, mid-day, dusk, night).
