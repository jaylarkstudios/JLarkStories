Hey, it's your buddy Lark! The real me, not some cleverly disguised drone. Cross my totally real and definitely beating heart! Anyway, the first quarter of 2022 is now dust in the wind. That went by way faster than I thought it would. But that means it's time to start planning for the second quarter!

Let me start by saying that things are going to be very busy for me on the home front. We have a lot planned this year in terms of home improvements, travel, and family activities. All this will very likely reduce my output over the coming year. As much as I love telling stories through art and comics, my family will always come first. Fortunately, they are very supportive of my artistic endeavors, so I have come up with some plans for managing my reduced capacity while still maintaining some level of consistency.

First, I wanted to talk about commissions. I've been taking more art commissions lately. This is because I enjoy doing them, but also because I've been taking the financial side of my art and comics business more seriously, and commissions are a good way to supplement my income. But I want to keep working on comics as well. Striking a balance between the two has been tricky. But what I'm going to try moving forward is divvying up my week and dedicating certain days to working on commissions only, and other days for comic work. And speaking of comic work...

Let's talk about *Aww, Feathers!* shall we? I appreciate those who've reached out to say they miss the comic and ask when the next chapter is coming. It's nice to know people like the story enough to actually notice when it's not there! Rest assured, it's not going away. I know this break has been longer than expected. Definitely longer than I wanted. However, as I was preparing to work on the next chapter, I realized that the writing needs more work. I'm not sure if anyone has noticed, but I've really been working on improving my writing skills lately. So while I could have started on the artwork for this chapter already, overall, the story would have suffered as a result. All this is to say, I'm going to spend the next month or two polishing the writing and spending more time planning out the next few chapters to make sure they all work well together. This means the next chapter probably will not be finished this quarter. But it will be worth the wait when it finally does come out. Thank you so much for your patience! In the meantime, I do plan on reformatting and reposting some of the earliest chapters in the comic, as well as working on some mini comics.

Speaking of mini comics, you may have noticed several comics I've released that feature characters representing not only myself, but also my wife and kids. I've really enjoyed making these comics, and the reception seems to be positive, so I plan on making more over the coming weeks. They don't take nearly as much time to complete as a full page of *Aww, Feathers!*, so they can help fill some of the gap while I work on other things. Even so, I don't think I can commit to releasing a new comic every week at this time. There may be times when I'm able to do so. But for now, I will aim for every other week.

Lastly, I wanted to wrap up this Q2 Roadmap by taking the things I've already talked about and solidifying them down into some concrete goals for the sake of accountability. To wit, by the end of June 2022, I want to have accomplished the following:

- Publish at least six mini comics.
- Reformat one old chapter of *Aww, Feathers!*
- Finish revising the next comic chapter.
- Write the script for the following chapter.
- Have a basic outline planned for the next three chapters after that.

If all is going well, then I also have the following "stretch goals" that I can try for:

- Have a basic story idea planned for another three comic chapters (following the three outlined above).
- Update the website homepage (I have a few improvements in mind).
- After finalizing the writing, start making progress on the art for the next comic chapter, and get it to the point where it's on track to be completed by the end of Q3.
- Start posting weekly reports on progress towards art and writing goals.

Whew! That was quite the ride! Thanks for sticking with me. This is going to be one heckuva year! While the plans I've made may be scaled back, they're still ambitious and are sure to be challenging. But I'm going to give it a good swing. Wish me luck!

-Lark
