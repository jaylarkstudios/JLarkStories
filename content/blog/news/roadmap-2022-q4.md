It's time already to plan the last few months of the year! Here's what we can see coming down the road:

**CURSEDtober Drawing Challenge**

If you haven't seen it yet, check out [this post](https://www.patreon.com/posts/introducing-open-72206525) detailing the drawing challenge I've created for this year! Busy as I am, I still wanted to participate because of how much I enjoy this challenge! However, this year I won't be doing a drawing for every day of the month. In fact, I plan on only doing a couple per week. So if you'd like a pen and ink commission from me, check out that post for details on how to reserve one. And of course if you yourself are artistically inclined, feel free to use the prompts provided to make your own scary creations!

**New Comic Update Schedule**

Over the years I've tried a variety of techniques to keep up a consistent update schedule for my comics, with varying levels of success. My most recent plan had been to complete the entire comic chapter before beginning to post it online. And while I still see that as the ideal situation, it has two rather unfortunate side effects: first, there's been very long gaps between chapters while I try to get the next one underway. This is exacerbated by the second issue, which is that I tend to work better with a clearer deadline for each page. So while I may work towards completing an entire chapter prior to release in the future, for now I plan to try something a little different.

For now, I lack the bandwidth to release a new comic page every week. But I can handle a schedule that's less frequent than that. So in the name of not letting the perfect release schedule become the enemy of the good release schedule, I'm going to *finally* begin updating *Aww, Feathers!* once again! The first page of the next chapter is officially scheduled to come out on **November 1st!** To start off, I plan to post one page per month. And that will serve as a bare minimum moving forward; no matter how busy life gets, I should be able to at least maintain that. Still, that may seem like a long gap between pages, and it is. But at the same time, that will allow me to start gradually refilling a buffer of scheduled pages. Once my buffer reaches a certain size, I'll increase the update frequency to twice per month. Finally, once I reach a point where doing so will be sustainable, I'll bump it back to updating every week. If life ever gets to the point where I'm unable to keep up with that schedule, I may put the breaks on and slow updates back down again. But this way, updates will come out much more predictably, and more or less consistently!

**And the rest!**

I'll continue writing the following comic chapter while working on the artwork for this next one. I don't know if I'll be opening any more general commission slots following October. If I do, I'll definitely let patrons know first, so watch this space. And of course we have the holidays coming up. I'm optimistic though that this new, more flexible update schedule will help keep things on track.

Thanks for taking the time to read!
