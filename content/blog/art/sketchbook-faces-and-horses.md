Been a while since I've uploaded a sketchbook page, but I've recently been getting back into the habit of drawing regularly in my practice sketchbook.

Kind of a mix of various subjects for this page. Near the top, there were a couple of expressions and poses from some furry Telegram stickers I saw that I liked and wanted to mimic. Then I finished off the chapter on faces from Chris Hart's *Beginner's Guide* book. One thing I picked up from this chapter is to pay closer attention to how the brow is shaped, particularly in the side view. It isn't just a smooth slope from the snout to the forehead as I tend to draw it. Rather, it indents slightly where the eyes are, and the angle of that indentation can vary based on gender, species, and even on an individual level. The book is full of generalities of how the male and female form *tend* to differ from one another, but of course those are rules that can be broken based on the look you want for your character. Female characters in artwork *tend* to have smoother, more rounded lines compared to their male counterparts, but you can easily give a woman a more angled, chiseled look, or a man a softer, more rounded look. Each one will have a different effect on the character and how viewers respond to that character at first glance. It may be a way of deliberately telegraphing aspects of their personality, or it may be to deliberately clash with the viewer's expectations. All these techniques are just tools to be used as the artist sees fit. The only real rule is to be deliberate about how you use those tools!

Lastly, I began a new chapter in Hodges and Cibos' book *Draw Furries* which focuses on equine characters! Horses are notoriously intimidating for artists to draw. Perhaps that's because they're so universally admired (I have a soft spot for them in my own heart too 😉). And yet there's something about the physical form of a horse that is, dare I say, beautifully alien. Their large eyes, their long, graceful neck, and their muscular bodies held up by slender legs on powerful and solid hooves. When you come right down to it, horses are probably among the oddest looking creatures that humans have domesticated, and yet they've been a part of our history for so long that they're instantly recognizable. So the drawing challenge is understandable, but not insurmountable. I'm certainly going to give it a solid effort. And it would, of course, be*hoove* me to do so! 😄🐎
