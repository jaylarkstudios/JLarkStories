> Courage is the price that Life exacts
> for granting peace.
> The soul that knows it not
> Knows no release
> from little things:
> Knows not the livid loneliness of fear,
> Nor mountain heights where bitter joy can hear
> The sound of wings.
> How can life grant us boon of living,
> compensate
> For dull gray ugliness
> and pregnant hate
> Unless we dare
> The soul's dominion?
> Each time we make a choice, we pay
> With courage to behold the resistless day,
> And count it fair. 

Amelia Earhart is probably most well known for her aviation career, setting numerous records including being the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean, and for disappearing while attempting to be the first woman to complete a circumnavigational flight of the globe. However, she was also a writer, not only of her experiences flying but of poems, such as the one above. I first heard this one as part of Christopher Tin's newest album, *[To Shiver the Sky](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bs_pwtXwXDE&list=PL2qQXyLK4-1X3kb0vis9vALN6CiznjEVc&index=8)*, and I've loved it ever since.

As we look forward to the new year, let us not grudgingly, but eagerly pay the price of COURAGE that we may soar to new heights above the gloom of hate, despair, and loneliness.
