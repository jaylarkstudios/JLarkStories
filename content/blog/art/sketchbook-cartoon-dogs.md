I recently checked out an ADORABLE book by Chris Hart called *Drawing Animals Shape by Shape*. (Not a sponsor by the way, just so we're clear!) I had ostensibly gotten it for my daughter, because it does a great job at breaking down animal drawings into very basic shapes, so an excellent place for young kids (or any budding artist, really) to start. But who am I kidding? I mostly got it for me, hehe. If you've been following my practice sketches closely enough, you know that I'm very interested in how to do more with less. More emotion, more structure, more movement, more energy, all with less actual drawing. Not because drawing itself is bad, but because I just really love the clean, streamlined look of many modern cartoons.

So anyway, my plan is to try my hand at the drawing exercises in this book, and then try out some of the techniques on some animal characters of my own invention. That said, I won't be using this book exclusively for my drawing practice. I'll probably switch back and forth between this and gesture drawing, which is a great way to get me warmed up to work on a comic page.

Anyway, that's all for now. Cheers!
