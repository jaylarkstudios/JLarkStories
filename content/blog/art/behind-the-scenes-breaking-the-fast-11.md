Boy, that new technique I picked up for drawing blushing characters sure came in handy for this chapter, didn't it! ☺

I realized in the middle of coloring this page that the background felt too empty. Ine is meant to be sitting off by himself, but it still made sense to draw at least one background character at the table behind him, not to mention all the others in the room that *should* be visible behind June! Luckily I had time to drop some in from a previous page. It didn't take too much extra work, and helped fill in some of that blank space. Sometimes blank space is fine; you don't always need to fill in the background details. But as I mentioned before, it's a balance. I try to develop a sense for when it's time to add more, as well as for when *less* is more.
