Hope you all had a great Thanksgiving! I thought it would be neat to try out drawing Ine's face as different species of lizards! For this one, I chose green iguana and Chinese water dragon!

In addition to all the "streamlining" I keep talking about, I'd also like to bring in more animalistic traits to my characters. I think this exercise might help. What are the physical traits that make each species of lizard unique? Which of those traits would be a good fit for Ine? Now you might be thinking, "Why not just use the traits for the species Ine already is?" The thing is though, Ine, like most of the characters in *Aww, Feathers!* is actually a mix. If you think about it, after centuries of different species mingling and having children together, it's not at all unlikely that one individual might have traits from several different species.

When asked his species, Ine's go-to response is "water lizard." Sometimes he'll be more descriptive and say, "red-feathered water lizard." His mom is a plumed basilisk (mostly that is, because like most anthros she too has mixed ancestry), and he got a lot of traits from her. And I'm sure in the future I'll try drawing him as a basilisk to see how well that fits. But if a certain lizard species' snout or jawline or other physical traits look good on them, I'll happily borrow from them.

SO THEN, which traits do I like or dislike on these two specimens? I actually kind of like the iguana's neck-flap (or whatever you call it). I'm considering giving Ine a little one of those. The shape of the water dragon's face is a bit more appealing to me though, but not so much the rest of the face.

I definitely plan on trying this exercise out again with more species! Also, I must give credit to [Reptile House BKK](https://www.facebook.com/ReptileHouseBKK/) for their image containing cartoon faces of dozens of different lizard species! I drew a lot of inspiration from that (I can't find a link to that specific image at the moment, but if I find it, I'll share it in a future post).
