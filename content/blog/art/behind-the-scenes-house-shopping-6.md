Nothing quite like that heart-clenching feeling that you've got no place to live for the coming school year. At least Ine is fortunate enough to have supportive family nearby he can fall back on if needed. Not everybody has that kind of plan B. (Of course, he's the more fortunate that he ended up not needing that option.)

You can see I experimented with a couple versions of panel 2. While the version with just his tail sticking out would have been easier to draw (cartoonists are lazy, lol!), the second does a better version of communicating what he's thinking, because it's clear that he's responding to something he saw on the computer screen, rather than something in the next room.
