We often see people drawn from the front. It makes sense. That's where the face is, so that's where we'll be looking most of the time when interacting with someone. But there's more happening on the back half of a person than we realize until we take the time to look more closely. There are muscle groups on the back, as well as places where the spine, ribs, and shoulder blades alter the shape and folds of the skin.
