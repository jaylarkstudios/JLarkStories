By the end of this month's art stream I was running out of time, but I decided to use what little I had left to make this simple tribute to a remarkable woman, Genie Chance, who became a voice of hope during the aftermath of the [1964 earthquake in Anchorage, Alaska](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/31/podcasts/the-daily/genie-chance-alaska-earthquake.html). Prior to the earthquake, she worked as a radio announcer for the sled dog race (hence the chosen species to represent this piece). However, after the earthquake, she used her broadcasting knowledge to get information out to those affected, give rudimentary survival instructions for things like water purification while the services were knocked out, put out calls for people who were missing or found, coordinate requests for needed supplies, and just help maintain calm during a time when mass hysteria could have easily erupted. In a later interview she simply said, "I realized... if there's any way I can be of service, I've got to do it." It may not seem profound, but her determination made a profound difference to the people of Anchorage, as well as people all over the world who would eventually hear her voice and learn her story.

You may not feel like there's much you can give, but there's always *something* we can do to be of service.
