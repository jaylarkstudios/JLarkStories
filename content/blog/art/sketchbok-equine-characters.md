My horse-drawing studies continue! It's interesting to try a variety of ways to portray hoofed characters. They can be given a more digitgrade stance in which they walk on two legs the way one would walk on tip-toe (the hoof is basically a large toe-nail after all), or they can be portrayed more plantigrade, with the hooves functioning like the bottom of the foot itself. And then there are the hands. The book I'm drawing from portrays horse characters with human-like hands, but with thicker nails, as if made from the same material as hooves. However, you can also give characters hoof-hands, which in turn can be a single hoof or split into a few hoofed fingers (almost like a mitten). This naturally raises questions about how such a character can pick things up and otherwise function in society, but it's not hard to assume an internally consistent world in which they've developed means of manipulating their environment.
