The first art piece to come out of this month's art stream was this one, with a quote from Valarie Kaur's talk, [3 lessons of revolutionary love in a time of rage](https://www.ted.com/talks/valarie_kaur_3_lessons_of_revolutionary_love_in_a_time_of_rage). It stood out to me, because if there was ever a moment of darkness felt almost universally across the globe, it would be right now. Still, this darkness doesn't have to represent a bleak and dismal end. All of us can choose to instead make it the precursor to a life renewed with purpose.

I chose a baby sea turtle to represent this theme because of how they are buried shortly after being laid. One usually associates burial with death. However, they are not dead, but rather simply waiting for the right moment to be born, to push and struggle their way upwards, and eventually emerge into the light and their new life.
