Arctic dragons, as the name implies, live primarily in arctic climates, as well as northern regions of the American, European, and Asian continents. They are also the only known family of dragons to grow fur. They have scales, and each scale produces a small tuft of fur, giving them their full coat. Their fur, in addition to keeping them warm in the harsh, cold climates they inhabit, also helps them camouflage. They are stealthy predators, known to hunt whales, seals, and even polar bears.

Dragons in Asian artwork often include dragons with fur. However, while arctic dragons are found in parts of Asia and share the serpentine body structure and lack of wings, they are not the same as Asian dragons (at least as defined by the *Dracopedia*). Falkor from *Neverending Story* is one well known example of an arctic dragon. Remember, the fur is the key defining feature!
