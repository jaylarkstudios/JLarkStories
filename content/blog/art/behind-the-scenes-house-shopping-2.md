If front-facing-Ine's head looked like a crocodile in the last page, it's kind of like a box in this one.

"Welcome to the real world!" Don'tcha just love it when adults say that? And so Ine continues to receive gentle nudges that it's time to grow up and learn to fend for himself. The job situation won't resolve itself, but that'll be addressed in a future chapter!

Fun fact: Ine's mom's face in the second panel is what I used for my own mom's contact photo in my phone for years! While it's not one-to-one, I do think about my own parents a lot when writing Ine's parents.
