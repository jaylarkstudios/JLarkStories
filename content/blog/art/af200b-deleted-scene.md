Here's a look at how the dialog changed for this page. The top right shows Chris' original line, and the bottom right shows the way it is now. I think it's interesting how such a slight word change (from "Thanks, but I already ate," to "No thanks, I already ate,") causes the latter to sting more; beginning with the word "no" makes it feel more like a rejection which does a better job of driving home June's feelings of disappointment in the final panel of this page.
