I've been working on streamlining, simplifying, and consist...ant...ifying...? Anyway, I feel I need to work on drawing my characters more consistently so they don't change from page to page so much. To go along with that, I've been trying to streamline and simplify my characters, not to the point that they lose the traits that make them unique, but rather to draw more attention to those traits.

Take Ine for example. His most unique characteristic is his crest of red feathers on his head and the tuft on his tail. Those two traits should be the most prominent and eye-catching. On the other hand, things like, say, his hands, feet, legs, etc. can afford to be simplified.

I think I've still got a ways to go. The front view of the face still gives me trouble sometimes. But I've heard the advice that goes, "Draw it until it stops changing." I'll keep working on that!
