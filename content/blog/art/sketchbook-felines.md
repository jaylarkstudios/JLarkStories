You didn't think I was giving up the study of drawing furries in favor of drawing hoomans, did you? While I am going to continue referencing Chris Hart's excellent books on drawing human anatomy, I'll also be drawing from the delightful series by Hodges and Cibos on drawing anthropomorphic characters, the first of which is aptly titled, *Draw Furries*! The first chapter is on, you guessed it, felines!

Cats have historically been somewhat challenging for me to draw. I think it might have something to do with their relatively short muzzle which causes the fact to look (when I try to draw them at least) a little too human, which in turn causes the end result to be somewhat uncanny. The key then is to accentuate the traits that make feline characters unique, which this book does an excellent job of demonstrating.

(Do you think the creators of the film *Cats* read this book...? If so, maybe they skipped this chapter.)
