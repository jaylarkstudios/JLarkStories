You ever run into that problem where you're drawing a character of the "split-lip" family, and no matter what you do, it always looks like they're smiling? Their upper lip always makes that lowercase w shape, which in turn makes it look like the edges are always curled up into a smile. >w<

That's always been a frustration of mine whenever drawing canine or feline characters. How do you get around it while still keeping the distinctly canine/feline quality of the character's face? I've had moderate success in the past, but it's very easy for me to fall back into that trap if I'm not very deliberate about the way I'm drawing. My recent studies of Chris Hart's cartooning books has helped tremendously, and enabled me to figure out some of the expressions you see above. As always, more polish is needed, but progress is progress! Ol' Chris is looking pretty good! And confused. And terrified. And bored. And flirty...

Oh, by the way, if you want to study from somebody who not only *absolutely nails* expressions but does so on *feline characters* to boot, check out [this tutorial](https://www.deviantart.com/tracyjb/art/Lackadaisy-Expressions-193978013) on the subject by Tracy Butler, creator of the phenomenally illustrated webcomic "Lackadaisy Cats!"
