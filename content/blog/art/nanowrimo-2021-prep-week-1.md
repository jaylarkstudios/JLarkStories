I've been following the schedule and exercises for preparing for NaNoWriMo this year. Even though I already have an idea in mind of what to write about, I still followed the prompts for generating new ideas, which was enjoyable all on its own. I even came up with some ideas I may toy with in the future. Feel free to take a look at my notes from week 1!

I am reminded of an assertion I've heard professional creators make time and time again: "Ideas are worthless; it's the execution that matters." So very often we find ourselves paralyzed by either the lack of a good idea, or by having too many ideas to pick from. But as long as it's an idea we can get excited about and one that we're willing to put the work into, virtually any idea can become a compelling story!
