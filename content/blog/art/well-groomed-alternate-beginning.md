Here you can see how the recent chapter was *originally* going to go. It was going to start with Ine in the steam room, and then realizing he was locked out, and finally knocking on June's door and being allowed inside. In the [final version](https://awwfeathers.com/comics/page219/), I switched things around a bit. It was a much stronger beginning to the chapter to start with what was originally going to be the third page. In the sequence above, the reader can already guess that it's Ine knocking on the door, because they saw him get locked out of his apartment before. In the final version however, there's no hint that it's actually Ine, so the reader is just as surprised to see him (in a swim suit, in the snow) as June is!

You may be familiar with the term *in medias res*, which literally means, "into the middle of things." It's a narrative device in which a story begins in the middle instead of the beginning, bypassing the exposition at first, and then gradually filling it in later through dialog, flashbacks, etc. It's a very useful way to hook the reader by showing something exciting or unexpected *first*, so that they become invested in sticking around to find out what lead to it in the first place. The result is that you convey the same information, but in a more interesting and engaging way by simply switching up the order in which it's presented!
