Basilisks (not to be confused with the tropical lizard of the same name) are found throughout the desert regions of North America, the Middle East, and Australia, as well as a few islands of the Pacific. They have long, serpentine bodies with many limbs. The largest can grow up to twelve feet long, but there are also must smaller varieties called salamander basilisks that are no longer than a foot, and inhabit active volcanoes. According to legend, they have the ability to petrify victims with only their gaze. However, in reality, they have glands near their many sets of false eyes that can spray a toxin that paralyzes prey. Their bite also contains the same venom.

Basilisks are adept at burrowing through desert sands and sensing movement on the surface. They are dangerous to anyone traveling through the desert alone.
