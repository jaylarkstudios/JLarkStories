How the heck do you draw a dolphin's face from the front? I've attempted it a few times over the course of *Aww, Feathers!* with varied results. But I think I'm finally getting somewhere! I'll be focusing on June a bit over the next few days of my practice sketches because (spoiler alert) she's one of the characters who will feature prominently in the upcoming chapter that I'm currently working on.

I think a few key things to get right about her face in the front view are keeping her chin and the curve defining the top of her "beak" low on her face, keeping the shape of her face fairly circular rather than a wide oval, connecting the top of her cheekbones to the line that defines the top of her head, and leaving enough space between the top of her eyes and the top of her head.
