In a continual effort to incorporate more animal-like features into my characters, I've been taking a closer look at how I draw June's face. Her mouth specifically. If you ever look at a picture of a real bottlenose dolphin's face in profile, you'll notice that they have a slight (or sometimes not so slight) underbite. Their lower jaw sticks out slightly farther than their upper jaw. I've always been hesitant to lean into that physical trait because I was afraid it would make June look goofy. And it certainly can if not done carefully. But I kind of like the way some of these are looking, especially the upper right one.
