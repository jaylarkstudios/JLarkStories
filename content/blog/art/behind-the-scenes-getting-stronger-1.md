Does your story have a villain? Does it *need *a villain? Virtually every engaging story is about characters who are relatable in some way overcoming some obstacle. They have a goal, but something is getting in the way of that goal, which creates conflict and narrative tension. That tension mounts as the characters repeatedly try (and often fail) to reach that goal and as the stakes rise higher and higher, until tension is released through a satisfying conclusion. (It doesn't have to be a happy ending; it just has to be satisfying).

Many stories employ a villain to personify the conflict their characters are facing. But this isn't strictly necessary. Many good stories don't have a specific villain. Sometimes characters are impeded by a force of nature, their own weaknesses and shortcomings, or even their friends or loved ones who, while not villains per se, have different goals that don't align with theirs.

*Aww, Feathers!* doesn't have many villains. A few notable baddies come to mind, such as Dil's biological parents for abandoning him, Luchette's ex for being unfaithful and manipulative, and the mean girls above for teasing Michelle in this and previous scenes. But most of the conflict has arisen from other sources. This may very well have something to do with my own personality; I'm rather averse to interpersonal conflict myself! And when it does happen, I'm much more inclined to assume that there's good in people, even those who aren't acting good. That said, there are times when writing a character or two who are more actively malicious serves the story better.
