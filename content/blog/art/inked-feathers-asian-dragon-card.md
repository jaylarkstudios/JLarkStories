The next family of dragons in the *Dracopedia* is the Asian dragon, sometimes referred to as the Eastern or Oriental dragon. As mentioned in the previous entry, Asian and arctic dragons are sometimes confused due to their somewhat similar body structure and the regions in which they are both found. However, Asian dragons do not have fur like arctic dragons, and they live in the more temperate or tropical regions of the Asian continent. In addition, Asian dragons have the distinctive feature of webbed fins along much of their bodies. These frills catch the air and allow them to glide short distances, giving rise to the myth that these dragons are capable of flight in spite of their lack of wings.

Asian dragons are known throughout the regions in which they live as symbols of luck, prosperity, and nobility, and some of the smaller species have even been kept as pets by those in the ruling classes.
