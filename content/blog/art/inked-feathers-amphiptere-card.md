So funny story... I've actually misplaced a bunch of my traditional art supplies, including the paper I use to create Inked Feathers entries. This is partly why it's been a while since the last entry (but only partly 😅). My work space is in need of some good organization anyway, so Spring Cleaning will probably come early this year, and I'm sure my supplies will turn up in the process.

In the meantime though, I did find a bunch of artist trading cards I got forever ago. I drew on a few of them, but then forgot about the rest... until now! So for the time being, I'll use these cards to draw on, and I decided to visit one of my favorite subjects in art: dragons!

The first entry is the amphiptere! Amphipteres have the iconic bat-like dragon wings, but no other limbs to speak of. So they're basically large, winged serpents. Amphiteres occasionally appear in European heraldry, but are otherwise not nearly as well-known as some of their more famous cousins. According to William O'Connor's *Dracopedia*, amphiteres are on the smaller end of the spectrum, averaging between 4 and 5 feet in wingspan. They can be tamed and kept as pets, and are useful for keeping rodent populations under control. However, wild amphiteres can be a nuisance and have been known to raid poultry farms, sometimes even fertilizing the eggs, resulting in the hybrid creature known as the cockatrice. So be sure to check your local laws and regulations before attempting to adopt one, and *never* release one back into the wild!

This was a fun exercise! Artist cards are only 2.5" x 3.5", so it's important to effectively utilize the available space.
